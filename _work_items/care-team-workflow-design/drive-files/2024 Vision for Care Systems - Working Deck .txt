A centralized work surface

Quick access to the context and tools care teams need to get their work done.


Problem - Care teams have to navigate to too many different surfaces to gather information about work to be done, and then to document and execute their work (notes, tools, appointments, etc.)
Vision - Provide a single surface that brings together all of the context and tools the care team needs to get their work done.

Automated task progress tracking

At-a-glance visibility into what work has been completed and what‚Äôs outstanding. 
Problem - It‚Äôs difficult for care teams to understand where a member is in a multistep process, what work is done, and what work still needs to be done. 

Vision - Commons provides at-a-glance visibility into all work that has been completed and all work that‚Äôs still outstanding to ensure care teams are delivering the highest quality of care to all of our members, every time.

Dynamic workflow tasks

Customizable work instruction based on market, payor, risk profile, and more.
Problem - Workflows prescribe a one-size-fits all process to care teams, forcing teams to hold context in their heads about any process variations that are needed based on different member attributes.

Vision - Workflows are customizable and can provide dynamic instruction based on market, payor, line of business, risk profile, and many other member attributes when we need our care teams to follow different processes and take different steps.

A Lesson From Core Services Foundations

While our xfn team was strong to begin with, with Market Ops fully integrated. Embedding Libby into the working team accelerated our progress, influence, and decision making capability significantly. For any major company initiative - like regular check ins, we need market representation at the highest level. We need to set the expectation that these leaders both ‚Äúlead‚Äù (offer direction)  and ‚Äúdo‚Äù (do work to bring that direction to life) in order for these programs to be successful. The teams that matter most to get things done, are the explicit cross functional teams we design. 

For upcoming major programs we should ask the following questions, and then structurally enable focused teams: 
Who is the key leader driving this body of work?
What teammates across the entire organization do they need dedicated to get that work done?
