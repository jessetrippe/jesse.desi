A pilot allows us to test our hypotheses, build confidence for scale and change course when necessary
Why run a pilot? To ensure the changes we‚Äôre making have the intended positive effects. A pilot lets us check those effects in an evidence based way

What data will we collect? For each new feature or solution, we‚Äôll gather quantitative data to test hypotheses about whether those solutions make users‚Äô jobs easier, more efficient and effective

We‚Äôll supplement quantitative data with qualitative overlay (from user feedback, surveys) to deepen our impact and operational learnings

How will we use the data? Where quantitative data and user feedback show positive effects associated with new features and solutions, we will make any final adjustments and proceed with implementation

Where the data show no change or even unintended adverse consequences, we‚Äôll pause, diagnose, and redesign
