Reaching a member can be challenging, we want to make the most of their time once they are engaged. 
Bundling work is good for members. Members get fewer calls from us, but each one is more meaningful and comprehensive. We deliver more value to the member in every interaction. Also, bundling helps us reduce transfers between care team members to create a smoother, less confusing member experience.
Bundling work helps make things easier and more efficient for care team members. Teams will go from conducting multiple outreaches per member to bundling more outreach efforts around one regular check-in (for tasks involving outreach).

Regular Check-In

Our mission is to radically improve the health of marginalized communities at scale. Each check-in is a step towards that - we meet the member where they are by affirming their greatest concerns, making progress on open needs raised by the member or Cityblock, and set next steps and expectations on when the next interaction with the member will be.
Proactive care (closing quality gaps) allows us to support our members in staying healthy
Keeping members engaged in Cityblock services (regular check-in) allows us to reliably be there for members in the moments that matter - if they need help, if things change, and on their overall journey to stay and be healthy.

Care Plan and Goal Guidelines and Requirements

Creating SMART Goals for the Care Plan
Cityblock uses SMART goals for both long (>90 days) and short term goals (<90 days)
Specific: Details of what you want to accomplish; the more specific, the easier it is to implement
Measurable: Allow you to track progress
Attainable/Achievable: Coach members to set achievable goals; break goals into small steps
Realistic/Relevant: Is it important to the member? What impact will it have?
Time-bound: Ground the goal with a deadline
Example: Member expressed food insecurity, difficulty managing chronic health conditions, including hypertension and diabetes.

SMART Goal - Short term:  Member will increase food accessibility by completing a referral to food resources in the community within the next month. 

SMART Goal - Long term: Member will increase food accessibility by applying and submitting Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) benefit (formerly known as food stamps) application by [date]

Goals are developed based on results from the core assessment and member interactions
Goals are centered around the memberâ€™s own priorities
Goals are guided by the domains of need identified
